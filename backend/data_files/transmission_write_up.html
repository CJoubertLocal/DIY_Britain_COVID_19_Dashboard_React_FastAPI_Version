Transmission Rates

The first positive tests for COVID-19 were recorded in late January 2020 (Source). Over the following years, the nations of England, Scotland and Wales introduced multiple legal restrictions on movement ('lockdowns'), with the purpose of limiting the spread of the virus. The first lockdown was formally declared on the 23rd March 2020 (Source).

The transmission of the virus was recorded with a reproduction number ('R value'), which summarised how many additional infections were expected for each infection that occurred. For example, with an R value of 2, if one person is infected, two additional people would be expected to become infected too (resulting in three total infections, before considering additional infections beyond that) (Source).
